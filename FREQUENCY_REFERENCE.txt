1. IMPORTANT NOTES & BAOFENG LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

LEGAL NOTICE: This document is for RECEIVE-ONLY (scanner) use on public safety
frequencies. Transmitting on public safety, government, or other licensed
frequencies without authorization is a federal crime (47 USC 333). The Baofeng
is being used here as a scanner/receiver only for those bands.

BAOFENG FREQUENCY RANGE:
  - VHF: 136-174 MHz (some models extend to 65-108 MHz FM broadcast RX)
  - UHF: 400-520 MHz
  - The UV-5R CANNOT receive:
    * Low band VHF (30-50 MHz) - CHP frequencies at 42 MHz are OUT OF RANGE
    * 700 MHz band - P25 trunked system frequencies are OUT OF RANGE
    * 800 MHz band - 8TAC/8CALL interop channels are OUT OF RANGE
    * 900 MHz band

BAOFENG MODULATION:
  - Receives analog FM and NFM only
  - CANNOT decode P25 digital, DMR, NXDN, or other digital modes
  - CANNOT track trunked radio systems

WHAT YOU CAN ACTUALLY HEAR:
  - VHF fire dispatch/tactical (153-156 MHz) -- BEST results
  - CalFire operations (151 MHz range) -- BEST results
  - UHF police conventional channels (460-490 MHz) -- GOOD results
  - National interop channels (VHF and UHF) -- GOOD results
  - NOAA Weather (162 MHz) -- GOOD results
  - FRS/GMRS/MURS -- GOOD results
  - CHP (42 MHz) -- NOT receivable on Baofeng

NOTE ON POLICE: Many San Mateo County PD agencies still use analog conventional
UHF frequencies in the 480-490 MHz range for primary dispatch. However, the
county has a 700 MHz P25 trunked system that the Sheriff and some agencies use.
The conventional UHF frequencies listed here were confirmed active as of late
2024/early 2025 per RadioReference databases. Some agencies may have migrated
further to P25 since then. If a channel is silent, the agency may have moved
to the P25 system.

2. RADIO HARDWARE RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

RECOMMENDED BAOFENG MODELS (best to least):

  BF-F8HP (8 watt)
    - Best Baofeng for scanning; improved front-end filtering
    - 8W max TX power (irrelevant for RX-only use, but better build)
    - Better receiver sensitivity than UV-5R
    - ~$60-70

  UV-82HP (8 watt)
    - Dual PTT button design
    - Good receiver, comparable to BF-F8HP
    - ~$40-50

  UV-5R / UV-5R Plus
    - The classic, cheapest option
    - Adequate receiver for nearby transmitters
    - ~$25-35

  UV-5X3 (Tri-band)
    - Adds 220 MHz band (1.25m)
    - No advantage for public safety scanning
    - ~$50

ACCESSORIES:
  - Nagoya NA-771 antenna (significant improvement over stock antenna)
  - Programming cable (Baofeng/FTDI USB cable for CHIRP)
  - CHIRP software: https://chirpmyradio.com (free, open source)

FOR SERIOUS SCANNING, CONSIDER INSTEAD:
  - Uniden SDS100 or SDS200 -- full P25 Phase I/II, trunking capable
  - Whistler TRX-1 or TRX-2 -- P25 capable
  - RTL-SDR dongle + SDR# or Trunk-Recorder -- widest coverage

3. CHIRP PROGRAMMING GUIDE
================================================================================

A CHIRP-compatible CSV file has been generated:
  menlo_park_area_frequencies.csv

To import into CHIRP:
  1. Download CHIRP from https://chirpmyradio.com
  2. Connect Baofeng with programming cable
  3. Radio > Download From Radio (to get current image)
  4. File > Import (select the CSV file)
  5. Select channels to import, click OK
  6. Radio > Upload To Radio

CSV COLUMN DEFINITIONS:
  Location    = Channel number (0-127 for UV-5R)
  Name        = 6-character alpha tag (UV-5R limit)
  Frequency   = RX frequency in MHz
  Duplex      = blank=simplex, +=positive offset, -=negative offset
  Offset      = Offset in MHz (0 for simplex)
  Tone        = Tone mode: (blank), Tone, TSQL, DTCS
  rToneFreq   = TX CTCSS tone (for Tone mode)
  cToneFreq   = RX CTCSS tone (for TSQL mode)
  DtcsCode    = DCS code
  DtcsPolarity= DCS polarity (NN, NR, RN, RR)
  Mode        = FM or NFM
  TStep       = Tuning step in kHz
  Skip        = S=skip in scan, blank=include
  Comment     = Description (not stored in radio)
